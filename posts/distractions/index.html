distractions

Mar 1, 2021

Distractions are everywhere. As the years roll on, more and more effective distractions are created. They seem to be so wide reaching that being distracted is now considered a norm. Even hearing that someone is actively doing nothing is considered abnormal now. Boredom is often considered painful (not that it can’t be). But what seems to be a worse fate is doom scrolling and still bored. I see it all the time in myself and those around me. The “I don’t have anything to do, so I’ll just check twitter/reddit/youtube/email/app of your choice here”. I do this constantly, even in times of self imposed restriction, I’ll often just substitute it with other things. I can’t tell you how many times I’ve opened GitHub for no good reason. There are a lot of parallels between weight loss and trying to become untangled from this web of distractions. I’d say it’s one part self discipline and three parts environment. If you’re trying to loose weight, generally you don’t buy junk food, and you try not to go to the grocery store hungry either. It makes eating healthier the easiest option.
It’s a similar thought process with the newer age apps. Feeling depressed? Why not chat up a friend on Discord! Want to see some cats? Here are all the cats in the world all at once. Hungry? Just order your hunger away with a swipe of an app and an additional fee. As some of you might have come to notice in my writings here, I try to pose questions for you to reflect on. And by asking the following, I’m not suggesting we become a new age techno amish. But what level of convience is worth our free decision? I’m not attempting to suggest that someone has a gun to your head telling you to,“ORDER THE FUCKING FOOD NOW OR ITS A BULLET IN YOUR BRAIN”. Now that could be an interesting screen play, but I don’t think these things work like that. In a roundabout way we are influenced to keep returning to the apps for the instant gratification. You’ll see the argument everywhere that we’re “the same biologically as we were 100,000 years ago with new technology that’s smarter than our subconscious” or some deviation of that. It’s not wrong, it just feels like it’s subverting the core problem: what we allow into our lives. If someone said,“hey come try this meth it’ll help you work longer!” to you in 1938, you might not know that it could really fuck you up long term.

There’s plenty of related movements to attempt to “end” distraction, which however nice it sounds seems to always end up closer to a cult style following. Reading related books (titles omitted as to not be rude) seems to suggest that they are an almost “elitest” class, something that, honestly, only people in good positions can only afford to “try out”. Which gives it a feeling of “exclusivity” and “speciality” I think it doesn’t deserve. I think the trick is to find the balance of things that allows you to optimally create, without having to release attention on a regular basis.

With that said, I always have a drive to make more, but I’m still deciding on which mediums to present the creations. I think I’ll make a two way newsletter of sorts, where we can have a more bidirectional conversation. As an added benefit I can send out creations without having to post it to social media and the likes.

Maybe we should call this non-movement techno-atheism. Get it?
